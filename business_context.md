# Business Context: Customer Segmentation & Personalization Initiative



## 1. Company Type \& Market Context



The company is a mid-sized e-commerce retailer operating in the fashion and lifestyle sector, serving a broad customer base with diverse product preferences, shopping behaviors, and price sensitivities. The business operates in a highly competitive digital marketplace where customers can easily compare prices, brands, and experiences across multiple platforms.



Over the past few years, the company has experienced increasing revenue pressure driven by rising customer acquisition and marketing costs. Paid digital channels (social media ads, search, influencer marketing) have become more expensive, while returns on blanket promotional campaigns have begun to plateau. At the same time, consumer expectations have evolved—customers now expect relevant, personalized experiences rather than generic promotions.



Competition from both established brands and fast-moving direct-to-consumer players has intensified. Many competitors are investing heavily in personalization, loyalty programs, and subscription-based models to drive repeat purchases and customer lifetime value. To remain competitive, the company must transition from broad, one-size-fits-all marketing to a data-driven, customer-centric engagement strategy.



This project is positioned as a foundational analytics initiative to help the business better understand its customers and allocate marketing resources more effectively.

## 3. Core Business Pain



**“We are spending heavily on promotions but treating all customers the same.”**



Currently, promotional strategies are largely uniform across the customer base. Discounts and promo codes are frequently applied without a clear understanding of who truly needs an incentive to convert versus who would purchase regardless.



This approach is inefficient and risky because:

- **Revenue Impact:** Discounts are often given to customers who are already loyal, resulting in unnecessary margin loss.

- **Margin Erosion:** Over-discounting reduces profitability without guaranteeing incremental sales.

- **Customer Experience:** High-value or loyal customers may feel undervalued when they receive the same offers as infrequent or price-sensitive shoppers.

- **Missed Growth Opportunities:** Customers who could be nurtured through loyalty benefits or premium experiences are instead trained to wait for discounts.



If this issue is not addressed, the company risks:

- Continued increase in marketing spend with diminishing returns  

- Reduced customer lifetime value due to poor engagement strategies  

- Inability to compete with more data-driven retailers who personalize effectively

## 4. Strategic Decisions This Project Will Enable



By implementing a robust customer segmentation framework, this project will enable the business to make clear, defensible strategic decisions about how to engage different customer groups.



### Who Should Receive Discounts?

- Identify price-sensitive or infrequent customers who require incentives to convert  

- Use discounts strategically to activate or re-engage specific segments  



**Business Impact**

- Improves marketing efficiency  

- Reduces unnecessary discounting  

- Protects margins while driving incremental revenue  



---



### Who Should Receive Loyalty Rewards Instead of Discounts?

* Recognize repeat and high-frequency shoppers who value recognition over price cuts  

- Offer rewards such as points, free shipping, or early access  



**Business Impact**

- Increases retention and repeat purchase rates  

- Strengthens emotional loyalty  

- Grows long-term customer lifetime value  



---



### Which Customers Should Receive Premium or Exclusive Experiences?

- Identify high-value customers with strong purchase history and low discount dependency  

- Provide VIP experiences, exclusive collections, or priority services  



**Business Impact**

- Drives revenue growth from top-tier customers  

- Differentiates the brand in a competitive market  

- Encourages sustained high-value engagement  



---



### Strategic Value Summary

Through customer segmentation, the company can transition from reactive discounting to intentional, value-based personalization, resulting in:

- **Revenue growth** through smarter targeting  

- **Higher retention** driven by relevant engagement  

- **Improved marketing efficiency** and cost control  

- **Sustainable long-term customer lifetime value**



This project establishes the analytical foundation required to support more sophisticated personalization, loyalty, and growth strategies across the organization.
